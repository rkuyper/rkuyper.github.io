Construction of free modal algebras — an application of duality theory

Dion Coumans

In the previous PhD colloquium, Sam van Gool discussed Stone duality for Boolean algebras and highlighted its significance to the study of classical propositional logic (CPL). As was illustrated, one may obtain information about CPL by studying free Boolean algebras and their dual Stone spaces. In general, for a given logic, an understanding of its (finitely generated) free algebras, may yield powerful results about the logic.

We will discuss an extension of classical propositional logic, namely modal logic. The algebras related to modal logic are modal algebras, that is, Boolean algebras extended with an additional unary operation. Finitely generated Boolean algebras are finite and easy to understand. However, in the setting of modal algebras, this is not the case in general. We describe a functorial method to construct finitely generated modal algebras as a colimit of a chain of finite partial modal algebras. An extension of Stone duality allows us to give a concrete description of the algebras in this chain approximating the free algebra. This construction may be applied, for example, to decide logical equivalence of formulas.
