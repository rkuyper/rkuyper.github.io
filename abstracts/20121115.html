Probability Logic

Rutger Kuyper

We will study a non-classical logic by Terwijn in which the universal quantifier is interpreted as 'for almost all $x$' (in the sense of 'with high probability') instead of 'for all $x$', but in which the existential quantifier retains its classical interpretation. This way, one can decide if a formula holds in some model by amassing enough (but a finite amount of) evidence, unlike in classical logic. The mathematical theory diverges from the classical theory at various points and the proofs require different techniques. In this talk, I will introduce the logic, and I will discuss some theorems from classical model theory and explain the differences between classical logic and our so-called $\epsilon$-logic.
