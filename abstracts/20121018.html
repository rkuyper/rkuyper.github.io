Matrix-Valued little q-Jacobi Polynomials

Noud Aldenhoven

The concept of symmetry.

Last week we saw the definition of orthogonal polynomials. One of the main examples of orthogonal polynomials in this talk were the Jacobi polynomials. We also saw that there there is a $q$-analogue of the Jacobi polynomials called little $q$-Jacobi polynomials. In this talk we start with the basic definition of matrix-valued orthogonal polynomials, which are a non-commutative analogue for the (scalar) orthogonal polynomials. Then, using the theory of symmetric $q$-difference operators, a matrix-valued analogue of the little $q$-Jacobi polynomials will be given.
